The Learning Plan

A guided discussion on study goals, personal attributes and how I will find success during the Foundation and Bootcamp programmes

28 February 2025

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

Full stack developer. Long term I'd love opportunities to be involved with different industries and learn the role software plays in achieving their vision. I also admire small business. Down the road, I'd love to give back in some way by helping find software solutions that could help small businesses succeed.


Give a description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

Learning strengths:

  • Happy dancing. I find problem-solving enjoyable. Nothing is better than celebrating after solving what is seemingly impossible at the time.
  • I love a list! I'm checklist-orientated and find it helps with breaking a bigger goal into short-term successes and lets you figure out what you need to prioritize.
  • I'm a creative thinker. My work life so far has taught me the importance of being innovative. This can be in big and small ways but always lead by the same question: is there a way this could be done better?

Learning limitations:

  • Oh flip! I've only had more 'traditional' learning experiences up until now and the flipped classroom approach has meant forming new habits, moving away from knowledge and answers towards thinking critically and seeking solutions. It'll be a journey but it's been rewarding so far!
  • The keyboard. Like, the literal keys. I'm not the quickest at typing out code, I don't know all the shortcuts. But we're getting there...slowly.
  • Self belief. I find my confidence in one thing is often dictated by what's going in the rest of my life - a not so great week at work means it might shake my self belief during study.

What non-technical/human skills would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Collaboration. In a day job where I have a management role, it can feel very singular at times. I'm excited to see what I can learn working with others without this dynamic at play and learning from those with more background in coding.

Building relationships. Having been in retail, customer service, entertainment industries in the past, I'm looking forward to being introduced to, and working with, a new cross-section of people and personalities.

Managing uncertainty. I do OK but want to push myself in this regard. In an industry that's forever evolving. I want to develop strategies for when I feel 'lost' in learning and better manage that feeling of "am I ever going to understand this"?


Outline your commitment to managing your workload

I have scheduled my week to include twenty hours of study around work and other commitments. I have organized to have Saturdays 'free'. This provides a buffer and should anything unexpected come up, there is room to be flexible or add in some extra study time.

When working with others, I value their time and respect that we are all showing up with different things going on in our personal lives. While the best way to converse, brainstorm ideas or execute the workload may differ among the group, what will be consistent is my level of respect, an eagerness to succeed and, importantly, my aim have a great time learning to code! Any moments that aren't feeling so great, my preference is to talk it through as soon as possible and try find a solution. I'll seek advice from people outside a situation when needed and if really bothersome, there is the facilitation team to reach out to. One-on-one conversations, the discord chat or messages are all tools available to use.

If I've reached a difficult spot in learning, I will look to previous notes or to the internet for some additional resources. Following that, I will message my homegroup or the cohort at large to see if anyone else is feeling the heat too or has some guidance. If I haven't found a solution within 2-3 days, I will reach out to the facilitators.


What do you expect from the Facilitation team?

Nothing that hasn't already been demonstrated: a set time to check-in regularly with our homegroup, options for conversations throughout the week if needed, care for how we're doing and how our lives are outside of studying. Moving towards the more intense parts of the course, thoughtful feedback, suggestions for moving forward and a positive figure to call on for guidance is what I will find most helpful.


What other commitments do you need to consider during the course of your studies this year?

We are due to have a family reunion this year across Easter weekend. Apart from that, there aren't any commitments I haven't already accounted for. I usually work Sundays and have indicated at work, the dates we'll have weekend projects. Thankfully, my team are super accommodating and will help make this work for me.
