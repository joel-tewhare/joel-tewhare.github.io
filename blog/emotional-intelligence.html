Emotional Intelligence: a little research, a lot learned

4 March 2025

The title of this post is from one of my favorite poems by Rumi. It came to mind at the end of doing some research into emotional intelligence. It's been a worthwhile exercise finding write ups and videos that discuss this concept. It's something I work on but had never considered it from an academic point of view.

The biggest benefit I took away was that having great EQ means you're able to monitor your own and others emotions, then use what you learn to guide your thinking and actions. If we think of IQ as 'what you can do' then EQ is 'how you do it'. What's great is emotional intelligence is learned and learnable - like a muscle, you can take your EQ to the gym and work on it.

Some mental tools of the trade:

  • Mindfulness - being able to relate to others, and understand ourselves. As a result, we become less reactive in challenging situations.
  • Self-mastery - tuning in to who we are, how we are and how we engage in situations.
  • Creating useful mental habits - developing methods to deal with critical thoughts and judgement of yourself and others.

You're a winner when you have the right systems and practices in place to work at improving your EQ (think meditation, journal-writing for example). Not only are empathy, improved happiness and self-awareness nice qualities as a result, they are key contributors towards high quality performance, leadership and working at an optimal level.

So here's to improving our emotional intelligence along this journey and hopefully managing our thoughts and feelings in a productive way moving forward - a notion touched on in 'The Guest House' by Rumi. I really love this poem, and I love it more read by Helena Bonham Carter - check it out.
